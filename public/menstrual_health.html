Menstrual Phase

Days 1-5

Your period begins as the uterus sheds its lining. Hormone levels are at their lowest.

Common Symptoms:
Cramping Fatigue Headaches Bloating
Self-Care Tips:
  • Use heating pads for cramps
  • Stay hydrated and rest
  • Eat iron-rich foods
  • Gentle exercise if comfortable

Follicular Phase

Days 6-14

Estrogen rises as your body prepares for ovulation. Energy and mood typically improve.

Common Symptoms:
Increased Energy Better Mood Clearer Skin Higher Libido
Self-Care Tips:
  • Try new exercises or activities
  • Focus on creative projects
  • Socialize and connect with friends
  • Plan important events

Ovulation

Day 14

The egg is released from the ovary. Fertility peaks during this time.

Common Symptoms:
Peak Energy Increased Libido Cervical Changes Mild Cramping
Self-Care Tips:
  • Track basal body temperature
  • Monitor cervical mucus
  • Plan conception if trying to conceive
  • Use protection if avoiding pregnancy

Luteal Phase

Days 15-28

Progesterone rises to prepare for possible pregnancy. PMS symptoms may begin.

Common Symptoms:
Mood Swings Breast Tenderness Food Cravings Bloating
Self-Care Tips:
  • Reduce caffeine and salt
  • Practice stress management
  • Get regular exercise
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
